Doctors Without Borders (Médecins Sans Frontières)

Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders) provides medical care to people affected by disasters, conflicts, and epidemics. They are committed to providing impartial assistance, irrespective of race, religion, gender, or political affiliation. Médecins Sans Frontières was founded in 1971 in France by a group of doctors and journalists. Since then, it has grown into an international organization providing medical aid and humanitarian assistance in over 70 countries. How They Help Immigrants: Doctors Without Borders often works with immigrants and refugees by providing medical care, mental health support, and humanitarian aid to those in crisis situations. They may operate mobile clinics, offer shelter, and address the healthcare needs of displaced populations.

Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund (MALDEF)

MALDEF is a civil rights organization that advocates for the rights of Latino communities in the United States through litigation, advocacy, education, and community engagement. Their mission is to promote social change and protect the rights of Latinos living in the U.S. MALDEF was founded in 1968 in San Antonio, Texas. Since its inception, it has been at the forefront of legal battles to protect the civil rights of Latinos in areas such as education, voting, immigration, and employment. How They Help Immigrants: MALDEF has a long history of advocating for the rights of immigrants, including issues related to immigration reform, DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals), and challenging policies that discriminate against immigrants.

National Immigrant Justice Center (NIJC)

NIJC is dedicated to ensuring human rights protections and access to justice for immigrants, refugees, and asylum seekers in the United States. Their mission is to advance immigrant rights and provide legal services to those in need. NIJC was established in 1979 as a program of Heartland Alliance for Human Needs & Human Rights. It has since become a leading advocate for immigrant rights and access to legal representation. How They Help Immigrants: NIJC provides legal representation and advocacy for immigrants facing detention, deportation, or seeking asylum. They work to protect the rights and dignity of immigrants, refugees, and asylum seekers through litigation, policy advocacy, and education.
